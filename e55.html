Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunt (TIPS)

Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunt (TIPS) is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat severe liver disease complications, such as portal hypertension. This procedure involves creating a bypass between the portal vein and hepatic vein, reducing pressure on the liver’s blood vessels and improving blood flow. It is commonly performed to manage variceal bleeding, ascites, or other complications of cirrhosis. If you're experiencing severe liver issues, TIPS can provide significant relief by improving liver function and reducing the risk of life-threatening complications.

Description

The TIPS procedure is an interventional radiology treatment that provides a channel between the liver’s portal and hepatic veins. By redirecting blood flow, it reduces the pressure in the portal system, alleviating symptoms of liver disease such as ascites and bleeding from varices. This procedure is performed under local anesthesia, and while minimally invasive, it can offer significant improvements in liver function and overall health.

TIPS is particularly beneficial for patients with cirrhosis who suffer from complications like variceal bleeding or refractory ascites, offering a life-saving treatment option when other methods have failed.

The TIPS procedure involves using a catheter inserted through the jugular vein to create a shunt between the portal and hepatic veins, relieving the pressure in the liver and preventing complications like bleeding and fluid accumulation.

The primary equipment used during the TIPS procedure includes a catheter, ultrasound, fluoroscopy, and X-ray guidance, which help in precisely placing the stent and ensuring the success of the procedure.

The TIPS procedure offers significant benefits, including improved liver function, reduced portal hypertension, alleviation of ascites, and prevention of variceal bleeding. It provides relief for patients with cirrhosis and severe liver complications, improving their quality of life and survival rate.
