Hysterosalpingography (HSG)

Hysterosalpingography (HSG) is a specialized X-ray procedure used to examine the uterus and fallopian tubes. It is commonly used to evaluate fertility issues, check for blockages in the fallopian tubes, and assess the uterine cavity for abnormalities.

Description

Hysterosalpingography (HSG) is a diagnostic procedure that involves taking X-ray images of the uterus and fallopian tubes after a contrast dye is injected into the uterine cavity. This procedure helps assess the shape of the uterus and identify blockages or other issues in the fallopian tubes that could affect fertility. HSG is typically performed when a woman is experiencing difficulties in conceiving, as it provides valuable information about the reproductive system's structure and function. The procedure is usually performed during the first half of the menstrual cycle, after menstruation but before ovulation.

During Hysterosalpingography (HSG), the patient lies on an examination table, and a speculum is inserted into the vagina to allow access to the cervix. A thin catheter is then inserted into the cervix, and a contrast dye is injected through the catheter into the uterus. X-ray images are taken as the dye flows through the uterus and into the fallopian tubes. This allows the doctor to examine the shape and size of the uterus and check for any blockages or abnormalities in the fallopian tubes. The procedure typically takes about 15-30 minutes and is done on an outpatient basis.

The main equipment used during Hysterosalpingography (HSG) includes an X-ray machine, a catheter for injecting the contrast dye, and a speculum for the procedure. The X-ray machine captures the images of the uterus and fallopian tubes as the contrast dye flows through them. The contrast dye helps to highlight the internal structures, making it easier to detect any abnormalities or blockages in the reproductive organs. In some cases, a digital fluoroscopy machine may be used to provide real-time imaging during the procedure.

Hysterosalpingography (HSG) is a valuable procedure for diagnosing fertility issues and identifying potential causes of infertility, such as blocked fallopian tubes or uterine abnormalities. It is a relatively quick, minimally invasive procedure that provides crucial information for fertility treatment planning. The procedure can also have therapeutic benefits in some cases, as the dye used during the procedure may help clear minor blockages in the fallopian tubes. Additionally, HSG is often performed as an outpatient procedure, meaning no hospital stay is required, and the recovery time is minimal.
