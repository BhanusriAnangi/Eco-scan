Microwave Ablation

Microwave Ablation (MWA) is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat tumors by using heat generated from microwaves. The procedure helps to shrink or destroy tumors in various organs, including the liver, kidney, and lungs. MWA offers a viable treatment option for patients who are not candidates for surgery or have inoperable tumors. It provides a non-surgical alternative to traditional treatments, such as surgery or radiation therapy, with reduced recovery time and minimal side effects. Microwave Ablation can offer excellent outcomes in the management of certain cancers, especially when combined with other treatment options.

Description

Microwave Ablation is a cutting-edge, non-invasive procedure that uses microwave energy to generate heat and destroy tumor tissue. The procedure is commonly used for liver, kidney, and lung cancers, as well as benign tumors. The process involves inserting a thin needle into the tumor site, where microwaves are emitted to heat and ablate the tumor tissue. The result is the destruction of cancerous or abnormal cells, which helps to reduce tumor size and prevent further growth.

The procedure is typically performed under local anesthesia or sedation, and the recovery time is significantly shorter compared to traditional surgery. It offers a safer option for patients who may be at higher surgical risk due to other health conditions, making it a highly effective alternative.

The Microwave Ablation procedure involves inserting a thin needle or probe into the tumor through the skin or via a catheter. High-frequency microwaves are transmitted through the probe to generate heat, which destroys the tumor tissue. The procedure is guided by imaging techniques such as ultrasound, CT, or MRI to ensure precise targeting of the tumor.

The primary equipment used for Microwave Ablation includes a microwave generator, a probe or needle that delivers the microwave energy, and imaging equipment such as ultrasound, CT scans, or MRI to guide the procedure. The probe emits electromagnetic waves that produce heat to destroy the targeted tumor tissue effectively.

Microwave Ablation offers numerous patient benefits, including minimal invasion, shorter recovery times, and reduced risks compared to traditional surgical approaches. This procedure can shrink or destroy tumors without the need for large incisions, providing faster healing and less discomfort. It can also be performed on patients who are not suitable candidates for surgery.
