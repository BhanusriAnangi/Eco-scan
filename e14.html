Lumbar Spine MRI

Lumbar Spine MRI is a non-invasive diagnostic imaging technique that uses powerful magnets and radio waves to create detailed images of the lumbar spine, including the vertebrae, discs, nerves, and surrounding tissues, helping in the diagnosis of back pain and related conditions.

Description

Lumbar Spine MRI is a crucial tool for diagnosing conditions affecting the lower back. By using magnetic fields and radio waves, it produces clear, high-resolution images of the lumbar vertebrae, spinal discs, ligaments, and nerves, assisting in the diagnosis of issues such as herniated discs, spinal stenosis, and nerve compression.

The Lumbar Spine MRI procedure is non-invasive. The patient lies on a table that slides into a large MRI machine. The machine uses magnets and radio waves to create detailed images of the spine, which are then reviewed by a radiologist.

The MRI machine uses a powerful magnetic field and radio waves to generate detailed images. Special coils may be used to enhance image quality for specific areas of the lumbar spine.

Lumbar Spine MRI is a highly effective diagnostic tool that does not require invasive procedures. It provides detailed images that help doctors accurately diagnose conditions such as herniated discs, spinal tumors, and nerve compression, leading to more effective treatment plans.
