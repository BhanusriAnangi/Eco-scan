Interventional Radiology (IR)

Interventional Radiology (IR) is a medical specialty that uses advanced imaging techniques to guide minimally invasive procedures. It helps treat a wide range of conditions such as vascular diseases, cancer, and other internal disorders. Through small incisions and specialized tools, IR procedures allow doctors to perform treatments that were once only possible with surgery, offering patients quicker recovery times and fewer risks. Whether you need a biopsy, treatment for an aneurysm, or catheter placement, IR techniques provide a precise and effective approach to healthcare. In addition to its minimally invasive nature, IR plays a critical role in improving patient outcomes by reducing the need for traditional open surgeries.

Description

Interventional Radiology (IR) is a minimally invasive technique that uses real-time imaging to guide doctors in performing diagnostic and therapeutic procedures. Unlike traditional surgery, these procedures typically require only small incisions or no incisions at all, resulting in less pain, reduced risk of infection, and shorter recovery times. Common procedures include angioplasty, stent placement, embolization for bleeding control, and tumor ablation.

The IR procedure is quick, precise, and performed under local anesthesia or mild sedation. It is an effective and versatile option for treating a variety of conditions, from cancer to chronic pain management, often replacing traditional surgical approaches.

Interventional Radiology procedures involve the use of real-time imaging techniques such as X-rays, CT scans, or ultrasound to guide a catheter or other small instruments through blood vessels or organs to the target area. These procedures are designed to treat a wide range of conditions, including blocked arteries, tumors, and bleeding disorders.

The primary equipment used in Interventional Radiology includes imaging machines like fluoroscopy, CT scans, ultrasound, and MRI. These technologies allow the physician to guide instruments, such as catheters, stents, and needles, to the precise location for treatment.

Interventional Radiology offers benefits such as reduced recovery time, fewer complications, less pain, and a lower risk of infection compared to traditional surgery. It allows for the treatment of complex conditions in a minimally invasive manner, improving patient comfort and outcomes.
