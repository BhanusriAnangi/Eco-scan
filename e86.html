Prostate-Specific Membrane Antigen (PSMA) PET/CT

PSMA PET/CT is an advanced imaging technique used for detecting prostate cancer, including recurrence and metastasis, through the use of a radiotracer that binds to the Prostate-Specific Membrane Antigen (PSMA) found on prostate cancer cells.

Description

Prostate-Specific Membrane Antigen (PSMA) PET/CT is a cutting-edge imaging modality that provides highly sensitive and specific imaging of prostate cancer. This technique involves injecting a radiotracer that binds to PSMA, a protein expressed on prostate cancer cells, allowing for precise detection of cancer cells even in early stages or cases of recurrence. It is particularly useful for detecting metastases and guiding treatment decisions in patients with prostate cancer.

The PSMA PET/CT procedure begins with the administration of a radiotracer that targets PSMA, followed by a PET scan to detect areas of cancerous activity. The scan is then followed by a CT scan to provide detailed anatomical images, enabling accurate localization of cancer cells.

The PSMA PET/CT scanner combines Positron Emission Tomography (PET) with a CT scanner. The PET scanner detects radiation emitted by the radiotracer, while the CT scan provides detailed images of the body's internal structures.

Patients benefit from PSMA PET/CT because it provides highly sensitive detection of prostate cancer, including its recurrence and metastasis, with minimal invasiveness. It helps in planning more targeted treatments, improving outcomes and reducing unnecessary procedures.
