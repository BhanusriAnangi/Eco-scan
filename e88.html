Sialography

Sialography is an imaging technique used to visualize the salivary glands and their ducts. It involves the injection of a contrast medium into the ducts of the salivary glands, followed by X-ray imaging to detect any blockages, stones, or other abnormalities.

Description

Sialography is a diagnostic procedure that uses contrast material and X-ray technology to provide detailed images of the salivary glands and ducts. This procedure helps detect conditions such as ductal strictures, stones, and infections, providing valuable insights for treatment planning.

The procedure involves the insertion of a small catheter into the duct of the salivary gland. A contrast agent is then injected, and X-ray images are taken to observe the flow of the contrast and identify any abnormalities.

Sialography requires specialized X-ray equipment and a contrast medium that helps highlight the salivary ducts. A catheter is used for the injection, and digital imaging systems are employed to capture the details.

Sialography provides a non-invasive way to detect salivary duct stones, inflammation, or structural issues. It helps in diagnosing conditions early, guiding treatment options like surgery or medication.
