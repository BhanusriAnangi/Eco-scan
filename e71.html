Biopsy of Lymph Nodes

A lymph node biopsy is a procedure used to remove a small sample of tissue from a lymph node to check for signs of infection, cancer, or other diseases affecting the lymphatic system.

Description

A lymph node biopsy is performed to diagnose conditions such as lymphoma, infections, or metastatic cancer. During the procedure, a needle or small incision is used to obtain a sample of lymph node tissue. The tissue is then examined under a microscope to detect the presence of disease. Lymph node biopsies are critical in staging cancers and determining the best treatment approach.

The biopsy is typically performed using either a fine needle or an incision to remove a portion of the lymph node. The procedure is often guided by imaging techniques like ultrasound or CT scan to ensure precision. Local anesthesia is applied to minimize discomfort. Once the tissue sample is obtained, it is sent to the lab for analysis.

The procedure requires a biopsy needle or a surgical tool for incision. Imaging equipment, such as ultrasound or CT scan, may be used for guidance. The biopsy needle is typically hollow, allowing tissue to be extracted for examination.

Lymph node biopsy helps in diagnosing the underlying cause of swollen or abnormal lymph nodes, allowing for early detection of diseases such as cancer, infections, or immune disorders. It plays a crucial role in staging cancer and formulating an effective treatment plan.
