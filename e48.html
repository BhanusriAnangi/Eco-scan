Stereotactic Breast Biopsy

A Stereotactic Breast Biopsy is a minimally invasive procedure used to collect tissue samples from a breast abnormality, often detected on a mammogram. This biopsy technique uses 3D imaging to guide the physician's needle precisely to the area of concern, ensuring accurate tissue removal for further examination. If you have an abnormal mammogram result, this procedure can help determine whether the area is benign or malignant, providing vital information for diagnosis and treatment planning. The Stereotactic Breast Biopsy is a crucial tool in the early detection of breast cancer, enabling physicians to make informed decisions about patient care.

Description

The Stereotactic Breast Biopsy uses detailed mammography images to guide a needle to a specific area within the breast. This precise targeting allows doctors to remove small samples of tissue for testing, helping to diagnose conditions such as breast cancer, benign cysts, or other abnormalities.

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia, and although it may cause some discomfort, it is generally well-tolerated. The biopsy samples are then sent to a lab for analysis, and the results provide valuable information to guide treatment options.

The Stereotactic Breast Biopsy involves the patient lying on a special table while mammogram images are taken to locate the area of concern. Once the site is pinpointed, a needle is inserted into the breast to collect tissue samples. The procedure typically takes about 30 minutes to an hour.

The primary equipment used for a Stereotactic Breast Biopsy is a mammography machine, which captures 3D images of the breast. A biopsy needle is then guided to the suspicious area using these images, and a small sample of tissue is extracted for further analysis.

Stereotactic Breast Biopsy offers a non-invasive method to gather tissue samples from hard-to-reach areas of the breast. It allows for precise diagnosis without the need for major surgery, reducing patient recovery time and providing quick results that help guide treatment options.
