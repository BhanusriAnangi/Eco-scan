Radiation Therapy Planning (CT Simulation)

Radiation Therapy Planning, also known as CT Simulation, is a vital imaging procedure used to precisely plan radiation therapy for cancer treatment. This non-invasive procedure involves the use of a CT scanner to map out the area of the body that will receive radiation. CT Simulation is a crucial step in ensuring that radiation is delivered accurately, sparing healthy tissues and focusing on the tumor. It allows the radiation oncologist to mark the exact area to be treated, optimizing the effectiveness of the therapy.

Description

CT Simulation is a specialized imaging technique used to plan radiation therapy with great accuracy. During the procedure, a CT scanner captures detailed images of the patient's body, helping the oncology team to precisely define the location, size, and shape of the tumor. This critical data is then used to develop a treatment plan that delivers radiation directly to the tumor while minimizing damage to surrounding healthy tissue.

The CT Simulation process is painless and typically lasts between 30 minutes to an hour. The patient may be asked to lie still in specific positions to ensure that the area to be treated is properly aligned. The images generated during the simulation are essential for the next steps of treatment, such as creating a custom immobilization device to help the patient remain in the same position throughout their radiation therapy sessions.

The CT Simulation procedure involves the patient positioning in various angles while the CT scanner captures detailed images of the body. The radiation oncologist uses these images to plan the precise areas for radiation treatment. The process may also include the use of immobilization devices to ensure that the patient remains in the correct position during treatment.

The primary equipment used for Radiation Therapy Planning (CT Simulation) is a specialized CT scanner. This machine captures high-resolution images of the patient's anatomy and helps to map out the area for precise radiation delivery. In addition, various immobilization devices are used to ensure accurate positioning during treatment.

CT Simulation provides numerous benefits, including ensuring accurate radiation delivery to the tumor while minimizing exposure to healthy tissues.
