Breast Ultrasound

Breast Ultrasound is a non-invasive imaging technique that uses sound waves to create detailed images of the breast tissue. It is commonly used to evaluate abnormalities such as lumps, cysts, or changes in breast tissue that may be detected during a physical exam or mammogram.

Description

Breast Ultrasound is a diagnostic imaging technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to produce detailed images of the breast tissue. It is particularly useful in differentiating between solid masses and fluid-filled cysts. This procedure is often recommended for women with dense breast tissue or for further evaluation of suspicious findings from a mammogram or physical exam. Unlike mammography, Breast Ultrasound does not involve radiation, making it a safe option for women of all ages. It provides real-time imaging and can be used to guide biopsies or drainage procedures if needed.

Breast Ultrasound is a non-invasive imaging procedure that uses sound waves to produce detailed images of the breast tissue. The patient is typically asked to lie on their back, and a gel is applied to the area of the breast to be examined. A transducer, which emits sound waves, is moved over the breast to capture real-time images. This procedure is often used to evaluate lumps or changes detected in the breast tissue.

Breast Ultrasound relies on a high-frequency transducer to send sound waves into the breast tissue. The ultrasound machine captures the waves that bounce back from the tissue to create images. No radiation is involved in this procedure, making it safe for women of all ages. The procedure may also use a gel to enhance the sound wave transmission between the transducer and the skin.

Breast Ultrasound is a safe and effective method for evaluating breast tissue, especially for women with dense breasts. It is non-invasive, pain-free, and does not use radiation, making it an excellent option for follow-up exams or initial evaluations. It can help distinguish between solid tumors and fluid-filled cysts, aiding in more accurate diagnoses and reducing the need for unnecessary biopsies.
