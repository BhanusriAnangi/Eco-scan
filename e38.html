MRI Enterography

MRI Enterography is a non-invasive imaging technique that uses magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) to capture detailed images of the small intestine. It is commonly used to diagnose and monitor gastrointestinal conditions, particularly inflammatory bowel diseases like Crohn's disease.

Description

MRI Enterography is a specialized type of MRI scan designed to evaluate the small intestine and surrounding tissues. It uses a magnetic field and radio waves to create detailed cross-sectional images, providing high-resolution pictures of the bowel, blood vessels, and adjacent structures. This procedure is often used for diagnosing inflammatory bowel diseases such as Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, or tumors. MRI Enterography does not require the use of ionizing radiation, making it a safer option compared to traditional imaging methods like CT scans. It helps physicians assess the extent of disease, monitor inflammation, and plan treatments for gastrointestinal conditions.

During an MRI Enterography, the patient is asked to lie on a table that moves into the MRI scanner. The procedure typically begins with the patient drinking a contrast solution that helps improve the images of the small intestine. Once the contrast has been ingested, the MRI machine captures detailed images of the gastrointestinal tract. The scan takes around 30-60 minutes, and patients may be asked to hold their breath at times to prevent motion during the scan. MRI Enterography provides highly detailed pictures of the bowel and surrounding organs, helping doctors evaluate conditions such as Crohn's disease, tumors, and bowel obstructions.

The primary equipment used in MRI Enterography includes the MRI scanner, which uses powerful magnetic fields and radio waves to create high-resolution images of the small intestine. A contrast agent is often used to enhance the images and help better visualize the intestines. The contrast may be taken orally or injected intravenously, depending on the type of scan required. The MRI machine is typically large and cylindrical, and the patient is positioned on a movable table inside the scanner, which captures detailed cross-sectional images of the intestines and surrounding tissues.

MRI Enterography is a safe, non-invasive procedure that does not involve the use of ionizing radiation, making it a safer alternative to CT scans for evaluating gastrointestinal issues. It provides highly detailed, high-resolution images of the small intestine, which can be crucial in diagnosing and monitoring conditions like Crohn's disease and other gastrointestinal disorders. The procedure is comfortable and relatively quick, allowing for detailed evaluation of inflammation, obstructions, and tumors. Additionally, MRI Enterography can provide valuable insights into treatment planning and help doctors monitor the effectiveness of ongoing treatments without the need for invasive procedures.
