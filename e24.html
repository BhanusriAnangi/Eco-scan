Cardiac CT

Cardiac CT (Computed Tomography) is a non-invasive imaging technique that uses X-rays to create detailed cross-sectional images of the heart and blood vessels. It is commonly used to assess coronary artery disease, heart function, and evaluate cardiac anatomy.

Description

Cardiac CT is a highly advanced imaging technique that uses computed tomography (CT) scans to generate detailed images of the heart, blood vessels, and coronary arteries. This procedure is particularly useful for diagnosing coronary artery disease, assessing heart function, and evaluating the presence of plaque buildup in the arteries. Cardiac CT can also be used to visualize heart anatomy, including chambers and valves, without the need for invasive procedures. It is a non-invasive, fast, and highly accurate tool that helps doctors detect and treat cardiovascular conditions effectively. Additionally, Cardiac CT is often used for pre-surgical planning and monitoring patients with known heart disease.

Cardiac CT (Computed Tomography) is a non-invasive imaging procedure that uses X-rays to create detailed cross-sectional images of the heart and blood vessels. During the procedure, the patient lies on a table while a CT scanner captures images of the heart and arteries. The procedure is often used to assess coronary artery disease, heart function, and evaluate the structure of the heart's chambers, valves, and blood vessels. It provides valuable information to help diagnose heart conditions and plan treatments.

The primary equipment used in a Cardiac CT procedure includes a CT scanner, which employs X-rays to create detailed images of the heart and arteries. The scanner rotates around the patient, capturing multiple images from different angles. A contrast dye may be injected into the bloodstream to enhance the visibility of blood vessels and heart structures. A computer processes these images to create detailed, cross-sectional views of the heart, which are displayed on a monitor for analysis.

Cardiac CT provides a non-invasive, highly accurate method for evaluating the heart and coronary arteries. It helps detect coronary artery disease, plaque buildup, and other heart conditions without the need for invasive procedures. The procedure is quick and provides clear, detailed images, allowing doctors to assess heart function and anatomy. It is especially useful for patients who may not be candidates for traditional invasive heart procedures. Additionally, Cardiac CT is beneficial for pre-surgical planning and for monitoring the progression of existing heart conditions.
