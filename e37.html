CT Colonography (Virtual Colonoscopy)

CT Colonography, also known as Virtual Colonoscopy, is a non-invasive imaging procedure that uses CT scanning technology to create detailed images of the colon and rectum. It is commonly used for colon cancer screening and to detect abnormalities like polyps, tumors, or other signs of gastrointestinal diseases.

Description

CT Colonography, or Virtual Colonoscopy, is a diagnostic procedure that uses a CT scan to produce detailed 3D images of the colon and rectum. It is a non-invasive alternative to traditional colonoscopy for screening and detecting colon cancer, polyps, and other abnormalities. During the procedure, the patient’s colon is inflated with air, and a CT scan is performed to generate images of the colon’s interior. The detailed images can help doctors detect abnormalities such as polyps, tumors, or inflammation. While CT Colonography is a highly effective screening tool, it is typically used for patients who cannot undergo traditional colonoscopy or prefer a less invasive option.

During a CT Colonography, the patient is asked to lie on a table while a small tube is inserted into the rectum to inflate the colon with air or carbon dioxide. This allows the colon to expand, providing clear images during the CT scan. The patient may be asked to hold their breath briefly while the scan is performed. The CT scan generates detailed 2D and 3D images of the colon, which are then analyzed by a radiologist to detect polyps, tumors, or other abnormalities. The procedure usually takes around 10-15 minutes and is non-invasive, with minimal discomfort for the patient.

The primary equipment used during CT Colonography includes a CT scanner, which captures detailed cross-sectional images of the body. A special tube is inserted into the rectum to inflate the colon, allowing the CT scanner to get clear images of the colon's interior. The CT scanner uses X-rays to create images that are processed by a computer to form detailed 3D images of the colon and rectum. The radiologist reviews these images to detect any abnormalities or signs of colon cancer, polyps, or other gastrointestinal issues.

CT Colonography is a safe, non-invasive procedure that does not require sedation or insertion of a long flexible scope into the colon, as in traditional colonoscopy. It is a quicker and more comfortable option for colon cancer screening, with fewer risks and a faster recovery time. The procedure is highly accurate in detecting polyps, tumors, and other abnormalities, and is often recommended for patients who are at high risk for colon cancer or those who cannot undergo traditional colonoscopy. Additionally, since the procedure is minimally invasive, patients can typically resume their normal activities immediately after the procedure.
