CT Pulmonary Angiography

CT Pulmonary Angiography (CTPA) is a diagnostic imaging test used to visualize the blood vessels in the lungs. It is primarily used to detect pulmonary embolism (PE), a condition where one or more arteries in the lungs become blocked by a blood clot. CTPA uses X-ray technology and a special dye (contrast material) to create detailed images of the pulmonary arteries.

Description

CT Pulmonary Angiography is a non-invasive imaging procedure that provides detailed images of the pulmonary arteries. It is crucial for diagnosing pulmonary embolism (PE), which can be life-threatening if not detected and treated promptly. The test uses X-rays and contrast dye to highlight blood vessels in the lungs, allowing for the detection of clots or blockages.

During the procedure, the patient will lie on a CT scanner bed, and a contrast dye will be injected into a vein. The scanner will take images of the lungs and blood vessels, creating detailed 3D images to identify any blockages.

The CT scanner, equipped with X-ray technology and a contrast injection system, captures high-resolution images. The contrast dye allows for enhanced visualization of blood vessels and any clots that may be present.

CT Pulmonary Angiography offers fast, accurate, and non-invasive detection of pulmonary embolism. It helps in quick diagnosis, which is crucial for starting immediate treatment and preventing complications.
