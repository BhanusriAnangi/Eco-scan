Percutaneous Vertebroplasty

Percutaneous Vertebroplasty is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat compression fractures in the spine, typically caused by osteoporosis. The procedure involves injecting a special bone cement into the fractured vertebra to stabilize it and relieve pain. If you suffer from vertebral fractures that result in severe back pain, this procedure may offer a safe and effective treatment option. Vertebroplasty is often recommended when conservative treatments, such as medication or physical therapy, have not provided sufficient relief. This procedure not only reduces pain but can also improve mobility and quality of life for patients suffering from vertebral compression fractures.

Description

Percutaneous Vertebroplasty is a medical procedure that targets spinal compression fractures, which are often caused by weakened vertebrae due to conditions like osteoporosis. During the procedure, a bone cement is injected into the fractured vertebra to provide stabilization and pain relief.

This procedure is minimally invasive and performed under local anesthesia, with a needle inserted into the affected vertebra. It is typically done on an outpatient basis and offers a quicker recovery time compared to traditional surgery.

The Percutaneous Vertebroplasty procedure involves the patient being positioned to allow access to the fractured vertebra. A needle is inserted into the vertebra through a small incision, and bone cement is injected under fluoroscopic guidance. The procedure typically takes about 30 to 60 minutes.

The primary equipment used for Percutaneous Vertebroplasty includes fluoroscopy (a type of X-ray for real-time imaging) and specialized needles for cement injection. The bone cement is typically polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA), which hardens quickly to stabilize the fractured vertebra.

Percutaneous Vertebroplasty offers benefits such as rapid pain relief, minimal recovery time, and a reduced risk of complications compared to traditional surgery. It helps stabilize the vertebrae, providing lasting relief from pain caused by spinal compression fractures.
